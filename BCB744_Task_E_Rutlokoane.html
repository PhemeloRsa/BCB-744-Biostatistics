The faithful dataset contains data on the waiting time between eruptions and the duration of the eruption for the old Faithful geyser in Yellowstone National Park, Wyoming, USA. Looking at the eruption duration, I used ggplot to plot the histogram of eruption durations and the waiting times.

1. Eruption Duration

The plot shows the histogram being clustered on the left (around 2 minutes) and on the right (around 4.2 minutes), with little eruptions occuring for 2.5 to 3.2 minutes. The measures of central tendency show that there is a negative skewness (-0.4135), which suggests that there is little asymmetry, where there is a longer left tail. There is also a negative kurtosis (-1.5116), which suggests that there is a flat distribution as compared to a normal curve

2. Waiting Time

The plot shows the histogram for waiting times. It shows that the waiting times are more frequent around 75 to 83 minutes. The measures of central tendency also show that there is a negative skewness (-0.4140). The kurtosis is also negative (-1.1563), which also suggests a flattened, spread-out distribution that is also asymmetric.

The rationale behind this approach was to divide the data into four equal parts based on the quantiles of the data using the quantile ( ) function. Then I counted the number of observations in each quantile interval by using expressions that filter the data ((0-0.25: counts <= 25%Q). I then converted the counts to percentages by dividing by the number of observations and multiplying by 100. This allowed me to see how the data was distributed and how it was spread out across the different quantiles. After that, I visualised the distribution of the data using a histogram.

Based on the box plots that were drawn, Chinstrap penguins have the deepest bills likely due to them being more suited for grasping and handling larger prey, followed by Adelie, while Gentoo penguins have the shallowest. Although the Adelie penguins have the highest maximum value for bill depth and some outliers, their 3rd quantile is lower than that of Chinstrap penguins. The Chinstrap penguins have the longest bills, followed by Gentoo, while Adélie penguins have the shortest. The separation among species indicates distinct bill morphology among the species. Gentoo penguins have longer flippers compared to the other species likely because they dive deeper and forage on larger prey, requiring stronger swimming adaptations. Chinstrap and Adélie penguins have similar flipper lengths, but Adélie penguins tend to have slightly shorter ones. Gentoo penguins are the heaviest, with their body mass clearly higher than Adélie and Chinstrap penguins. Adélie and Chinstrap penguins have overlapping body masses, though Adélie penguins appear to be slightly lighter on average.
